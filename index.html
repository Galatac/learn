Learn Galatac

An Introductory Course

This learning course is inspired by Margarita Madrigal and Language Transfer.

With very little grammatical explanation, you will begin to internalize the language. Don't try to memorize anything! Just take your time and really think through how to translate the phrases. And mistakes are ok! Each lesson will build upon what you learned before it. Think of this course like a puzzle. You are building neural pathways :)

Lesson 1

The word for I is... mi (pronounced like "me")

The word for to want is gwel

When you want to use a verb in the present tense, you add an "a".

The verb comes before the subject in Galatac.

How would you say the following? Think about it and then click to see the answer. You won't learn if you just click right away though!

I want.

Gwela mi.

The word for you is ti (pronounced like "tee")

You want.

Gwela ti.

The word for to see is apis (pronounced like "apiece")

I see.

Apisa mi.

You see.

Apisa ti.

To negate a verb, you place ne (pronounced like "neh") before it.

I don't want.

Ne gwela mi.

You don't see.

Ne apisa ti.

The word for that is se (pronounced like "seh")

In Galatac, the object goes after the subject. Take your time with this one.

I see that.

Apisa mi se.

You want that.

Gwela ti se.

Can you guess how to say the following?

I don't want that.

Ne gwela mi se.

You don't see that.

Ne apisa ti se.

In this lesson, you have learned:

  • mi
  • ti
  • gwel
  • apis
  • ne
  • se

Lesson 2

To ask a question, keep the word order the same as a statement, just raise your intonation.

Do you want that?

Gwela ti se?

The word for yes is gwi

Just think of the French "oui".

Yes, I want that.

Gwi, gwela mi se.

Take your time with the following!

You don't see that?

Ne apisa ti se?

Gwel and Apis are verb roots. Remember how we added the -a to verb roots for the present tense?

To say "I want to see", you just use the verb root for the second verb.

I want to see.

Gwela mi apis.

The word for of is u

When you include an object after a second verb, you need to make it the genitive form of that object. For most things, that means just putting "of" in front of it.

You want to see that.

You want to see (of that).

Gwela ti apis u se.

I don't want to see that.

Ne gwela mi apis u se.

The word for but is esu.

I see that, but I don't want to.

Apisa mi se, esu ne gwela mi.

In this lesson, you have learned:

  • (How to make questions)
  • gwi
  • u
  • esu

Lesson 3

The past tense is expressed by putting re before the verb root.

It's sort of like the English word "did" before a verb.

I saw. / I did see.

Re apis mi.

You wanted that.

Re gwel ti se.

You saw me.

Re apis ti mi.

I want you.

Gwela mi ti.

Remember how we needed to use "u se" instead of just "se"?

The word for of me is imi

Take your time with this one!

You wanted to see me.

Re gwel ti apis imi.

The word for of you is iti

I wanted to see you!

Re gwel mi apis iti.

Did you see that?

Re apis ti se?

The word for no is the same as verb negation. ne

No, I saw you.

Ne, re apis mi ti.

I saw you, but I wanted to see that.

Re apis mi ti, esu re gwel mi apis u se.

In this lesson, you have learned:

  • re
  • imi
  • iti

Lesson 4

You can speak in the future tense by adding -si to the verb root.

This addition takes the stress and emphasis. If the verb root already ends in an s, don't add another s.

I will see you.

Apisi mi ti.

You won't see me.

Apisi ti mi.

Will you want that?

Gwelsi ti u se?

I will see you, but I don't want to.

Apisi mi ti, esu ne gwela mi.

In this lesson, you have learned:

  • -si

Lesson 5

The word for house is tei

There is no indefinitive article like "a" or "an" in Galatac.

The verb for to buy is prin

I will buy a house.

Prinsi mi tei.

You can make words plural by adding -e.

I bought houses.

Re prin mi teie.

The word for apple is aval.

I saw an apple.

Re apis mi aval.

Do you want to buy an apple?

Gwela ti prin u aval?

No, I want to buy a house.

Ne, gwela mi prin u tei.

Do you see apples?

Apisa ti avale?

The word for and is ac (pronounced "ahk")

Yes, I see houses and apples.

Gwi, apisa mi teie ac avale.

In this lesson, you have learned:

  • -e
  • prin
  • tei
  • aval
  • ac

Lesson 6

The word for to go is ai

You go.

Aia ti.

You went.

Re ai ti.

The word for to need is rinci (pronounced like "reenky")

If a verb root ends in a consonant + i, replace the -i with -a for the present tense.

You need to go.

Rinca ti ai.

I want to see that, but I need to go.

Gwela mi apis u se, esu rinca mi ai.
